# Wylde — Business Architecture

This document is the source of truth for Wylde's business model, tier structure, partnership network, and operational approach. It sits alongside CLAUDE.md (which defines the product) — this defines the *business* the product serves.

**Operating principle:** founder-led premium transformation ecosystem that gradually productizes. Not a fully scalable platform built day one. The work, the testimonials, and the operational understanding earn the right to scale later.

---

## Strategic Frame

### What Wylde IS
A premium transformation ecosystem combining an AI-powered identity transformation app, structured group programs, and high-touch human concierge service — anchored by a curated network of clinical and wellness partners.

### What Wylde IS NOT
- Not a mass-market fitness app
- Not a marketplace platform connecting users to random practitioners
- Not a clinical SaaS product
- Not a content-and-community subscription
- Not a "scalable platform" trying to serve everyone equally

### The breakthrough framing
**The app is not replacing coaches. The app is the infrastructure layer.**

The app handles: accountability, tracking, reminders, engagement, identity reinforcement, AI guidance, transformation visualization.

Humans handle: wisdom, nuance, emotional guidance, interpretation, personalization, integration.

Wylde wins by being the only brand that holds both — the app makes the human service better, and the human service makes the app worth using long-term.

### Category positioning
"Premium transformation ecosystem with curated practitioner network."

Not "wellness app." Not "concierge platform." The combined frame is the point.

### What makes Wylde different
1. **Identity-first, not metrics-first.** Most wellness products optimize numbers (weight, sleep score, HRV). Wylde optimizes who you're becoming. Numbers serve the identity, not the other way around.
2. **Ecosystem, not point solution.** Most products do one thing. Wylde integrates training, nutrition, somatic work, clinical optimization, and identity work as one experience.
3. **Curated, not marketplace.** Practitioners are hand-picked by the founder. Trust transfers from Wylde to the partners, not the other way around.
4. **Human-first, AI-amplified.** AI scales accountability and reflection. Humans deliver wisdom and orchestration. Neither is replaceable.

## Three-Tier Architecture

### Tier 1 — WyldeSelf App
**Pricing:** Free or $9.99-29/month (TBD based on early user testing)

**Purpose:** Acquisition, engagement, habit formation, identity transformation.

**The user experience:**
- Daily journey loop (Identity Anchor → Morning Ritual → Training → Nutrition → Future Self Check-in → Close the Loop)
- AI guide (single unified presence, contextually surfaced)
- Future Self visualization
- Personal program (workouts, routines)
- Nutrition tracking
- Journaling and identity work
- Progression system (Ember → Forge → Steel → Wylde)
- Basic community access

**Positioning to user:** *"Your daily operating system for becoming your future self."*

**What it does for the business:** Top of funnel. Builds audience, establishes brand, demonstrates transformation, creates the user base from which Tier 2 and Tier 3 are sold.

---

### Tier 2 — Wylde Membership
**Pricing:** $299-599/month (final pricing TBD; starts higher, can flex down based on retention data)

**Purpose:** Bridge between self-guided transformation and full concierge support. The "I'm ready to go deeper but not ready for white-glove" tier.

**Includes four layers:**

**Human layer**
- Weekly group coaching (live, cohort-based)
- Monthly live workshops (somatic work, identity deepening, etc.)
- Office hours with Wilke or vetted coaches
- Community accountability (smaller groups within the membership)
- Access to Wylde Man cohorts (for men) or other identity-track cohorts as they develop

**Protocol layer**
- Guided 12-week programs (multiple flavors: foundational, men's, women's, longevity, creative-professional, etc.)
- Nervous system / regulation work
- Premium habit and identity protocols
- Custom protocol unlocks tied to user goals

**Partner layer**
- Discounted access to the Wylde practitioner network (rates negotiated per partner; typically 15-25% off retail)
- Vetted referral pathways for clinical/specialty needs
- Curated supplement and product partner discounts
- Future: meal delivery integration, recovery brand partnerships

**App layer**
- Premium app features (deeper analytics, advanced tracking, custom protocol unlocks)
- Async check-ins with a coach (limits TBD — possibly weekly/bi-weekly)
- Priority Future Self generation, expanded image options

**Positioning to user:** *"You've been doing the work alone. Now do it with people who know how to hold the work with you."*

**Honest positioning:** This is the sweet spot of the business model. High recurring revenue, scales beyond founder time, generates LTV without requiring 1:1 founder attention. The Tier 2 user is the long-term financial backbone of Wylde.

**Critical:** Tier 2 should feel categorically different from Tier 1 — not just "more features." The human and partner layers are the differentiation. If a user can't articulate the difference in one sentence, the tier is too soft.

### Tier 3 — Wylde Concierge
**Pricing:** $5,000–$25,000+ per engagement (typical engagement length: 12 weeks to 6 months)

**Initial structure:** Single Tier 3 offering. Founder-led, by application. Sub-tiers (Standard vs Premium) deferred until enough engagements have been delivered to know where the natural price split is.

**Purpose:** White-glove human optimization. Full integration of identity work, performance, clinical optimization, and life integration. The level of service that meaningfully changes someone's life over a 3-6 month container.

**What's included:**
- 1:1 transformation coaching with Wilke (limited capacity by design)
- Custom protocol design across training, nutrition, identity work, somatic integration
- Lab interpretation and integration into protocol
- Direct access (defined channels — text, voice memo, scheduled calls; not 24/7)
- Partner coordination across the practitioner network (peptides, hormone optimization, training, somatic, nutrition)
- Concierge accountability (the AI app handles structure, Wilke handles wisdom)
- Optional: in-person intensives or retreats (priced separately)
- Optional: travel/performance support for executive clients

**Capacity:** Founder-led, 3-5 active concurrent clients. Hard ceiling 8 (and only after operational systems are mature). Beyond that, Wilke loses ability to build the rest of the business.

**Positioning to client:** *"Everything you need to transform held by one person who knows you. The app is your infrastructure. Wilke is your translator. The network is your specialist team. You don't have to coordinate any of it."*

**Wylde Man as a Tier 3 track:** Wylde Man becomes a named flavor of Tier 3 — same price range, masculine-development emphasis, preserves the voice of the existing Wylde Man brand. A man can buy "Wylde Concierge" or "Wylde Man Concierge" — same service, different content emphasis. This consolidates operations (one Calendly, one pricing structure, one delivery system) while preserving the audience-specific brand voice.

## Cost Model — Tier 3 Economics

### What Wylde absorbs into the Tier 3 fee
- Wilke's coaching time
- Protocol design and oversight
- Partner coordination and orchestration
- Lab interpretation and follow-up
- Initial consultations with partner network (e.g., intro session with the somatic practitioner, nutritionist intake)
- Ancillary supplies that enable experience (needles, bac water, syringes for peptide protocols, etc.)
- Coordination tools and software

### What Wylde does NOT absorb (client pays partner directly, at member-discounted rate)
- Peptide compounds and prescription medications
- Hormone optimization protocols
- Standalone clinical visits beyond intake
- Major physical product orders
- Ongoing 1:1 sessions with partner practitioners after initial integration
- Lab panel fees (TBD per partner — may be absorbed if partner offers volume rate)

### Why this split is correct
1. **Liability and regulatory.** Wylde stays out of the medical/pharmacy chain. Clinics prescribe and dispense; clients pay clinics. Wylde orchestrates, not resells.
2. **Margin protection.** Peptide and prescription costs are unpredictable per client. Absorbing them eats Tier 3 margin alive.
3. **Clean contractual relationships.** Client has direct medical relationship with clinic. Wylde has service relationship with client. No commingling.
4. **Premium clients expect this.** $15-25K clients aren't trying to avoid paying for their peptides — they want orchestration and member rates, not bundled meds.

### Worked example — Tier 3 engagement at $15,000 (12 weeks)
- Revenue: $15,000
- Absorbed costs: ~$1,000-1,500 (supplies, partner intake fees, lab interpretation, software/tools)
- Wilke time cost: $0 cash (founder time, not a cash expense)
- **Margin: $13,500-14,000 per engagement**

3-5 concurrent founder-led engagements per quarter:
- **Quarterly revenue: $40K-75K (depending on engagement value mix)**
- **Annualized at full capacity: ~$200-300K from Tier 3 alone**

This is what makes founder-led Tier 3 the fastest realistic path to revenue. The Tier 3 numbers fund operations while Tier 1 and Tier 2 build the audience needed to make those tiers profitable.

## Partner Network

### Five-domain anchor structure

Wylde does NOT build a marketplace. Wylde builds a *curated network* — five anchor partner relationships covering the domains that matter for human optimization.

| Domain | What the partner covers | Wilke's current state |
|---|---|---|
| **Peptide / hormone clinic** | Compounded peptides, hormone optimization, biochemical layer | ✅ Partner identified, relationship in place, open to working together |
| **Functional medicine practice** | Labs, interpretation, root-cause clinical work | 🟡 Open — needs identification and outreach |
| **Performance training** | Physical layer, in-person training partner or facility | 🟡 Open — needs identification and outreach |
| **Functional nutrition** | Nutritional protocols, meal strategy, dietary optimization | 🟡 Open — needs identification and outreach |
| **Somatic / mental health** | Nervous system, emotional regulation, integration work | 🟡 Open — needs identification and outreach (high strategic value — most optimization brands ignore this) |

### Partner economics
Each partner is a separate negotiation. Standard structure:
- **Member discount rate** (Tier 2 members): typically 15-25% off the partner's retail rates
- **Concierge integrated rate** (Tier 3 absorbed costs where applicable): typically 25-40% off retail, in exchange for guaranteed referral volume
- **Co-marketing rights:** Wylde can name the partner in materials; partner can name Wylde in their materials
- **Referral attribution:** Both directions — Wylde sends clients to partners, partners send clients to Wylde for transformation work

### Critical discipline: don't over-partner
Three deep partners > twenty shallow ones. The first 6 months: lock the peptide partner formally, identify and lock 2 more (probably functional medicine and somatic — highest-leverage). The remaining domains can be filled in months 6-12.

### What's NOT a partnership goal yet
- Real estate / wealth advisor referral networks (later)
- Supplement brand co-development (much later)
- Meal delivery integration (later, after Tier 2 has membership volume to justify)
- Recovery brand partnerships (later)
- Multi-state clinical expansion (much later)

## Wylde Man Integration

Wylde Man becomes a **named track inside the Wylde ecosystem** rather than a parallel brand. Specifically:

**At Tier 3:** "Wylde Man Concierge" — masculine-development flavor of the standard Tier 3 service. Same price range, same orchestration, content emphasizes masculine work, brotherhood, embodied leadership.

**At Tier 2 (when launched):** "Wylde Man cohort" — masculine cohort offering inside the broader Tier 2 membership. Men's track + universal Wylde Self resources.

**At Tier 1:** No change. WyldeSelf app remains universal.

### Operational consolidation
- Wylde Man's existing pricing tiers ($333 single / $2,444 / $4,444 / $7,777) consolidate into Tier 3 pricing
- Existing Wylde Man clients continue at their current pricing through current engagement
- Future Wylde Man clients buy Tier 3 (Wylde Man flavor) at the new pricing
- Calendly: consolidate to one Wylde booking flow with intent selector ("I'm interested in: Wylde Man Concierge / General Concierge / Membership inquiry / Partnership inquiry")
- Wylde Man brand voice and content remain — it's a track, not a separate brand
- wyldeman.net continues to operate as a marketing surface that funnels into the same Wylde ecosystem

### Why this works
- Preserves Wylde Man's audience and voice (which has real equity)
- Stops running two parallel brands with parallel operations
- Creates clean upgrade path: Wylde Man free guide → Wylde Self app → Wylde Membership → Wylde Man Concierge
- New men's audiences can enter via either brand; the ecosystem holds them either way

## Funnel Mechanics

The funnel runs in this order:

```
1. Marketing surfaces           (organic content, partnerships, founder presence)
       ↓
2. Tier 1: WyldeSelf app        (free or low-cost, builds audience)
       ↓
3. Tier 2: Wylde Membership     (recurring revenue, cohort experience)
       ↓
4. Tier 3: Wylde Concierge      (high-touch, transformation, founder-led)
```

### How users move between tiers

**Tier 1 → Tier 2 conversion:**
- Surfaced inside the app at moments of identity progression (e.g., reaching a new phase: Ember → Forge)
- Surfaced when user demonstrates engagement (streaks, completed cohorts of free content)
- Email/notification campaigns timed to user-state moments
- Founder content (podcast, social, newsletter) drives toward membership

**Tier 2 → Tier 3 conversion:**
- Tier 3 is BY APPLICATION ONLY (this is critical — it's part of the positioning)
- Tier 2 members can apply after a minimum engagement period (e.g., 3-6 months of membership)
- Tier 3 spots open intentionally rarely (3-5 capacity reinforces scarcity authenticity)
- Application reviewed by Wilke; not everyone gets in

**External direct-to-Tier-3:**
- High-net-worth referrals from existing partners (e.g., a peptide clinic refers a client to Wylde Concierge)
- Founder network — people who already know Wilke
- These bypass Tier 2 if they're ready and qualified

## Operational Model

### Phase 1 (months 0-3): Founder-led, no employees
- Wilke delivers all Tier 3 engagements personally
- All partner relationships are founder-managed
- No hires. Use contractors for specific deliverables (designer, video editor, etc.) on project basis only
- Tier 2 not yet launched — focus is Tier 3 revenue + Tier 1 app build

### Phase 2 (months 3-9): Tier 2 launches; first contractors
- First Tier 2 cohort launches with Wilke as lead facilitator
- Possibly: contract a guest facilitator for one workshop per month
- Possibly: contract a part-time community manager (rev share or fixed fee, not employee)
- Tier 3 capacity remains 3-5 with Wilke

### Phase 3 (months 9-18): First non-founder coach delivery
- One additional coach trained to deliver a *subset* of Tier 3 engagements (lower-priced range, e.g., $5-8K offerings)
- Wilke retains the elite tier ($15-25K+)
- Coach is a contractor with rev share, not an employee
- Tier 2 cohorts can be co-led

### Long-term (months 18+): Selective scaling
- Coach certification program is explicitly NOT pursued in year 1 — it's a separate product with its own complexity
- Scale comes from: 2-3 trained Wylde-aligned coaches operating under contractor agreements; Tier 2 reaching 200-500 members; partner network expansion
- Employees considered only when revenue is consistently high enough to justify and operations clearly need them

### What we explicitly do NOT do in year 1
- Build a coach certification track
- Build proprietary supplement line
- Build a marketplace platform for arbitrary practitioners
- Hire full-time employees
- Take on retail / e-commerce operations
- Multi-state clinical expansion

## Strategic Risks (worth naming)

### Founder dependency
The entire model rests on Wilke. If Wilke is unable to deliver Tier 3 (illness, burnout, life event), the business stalls. Mitigation: documenting protocols and engagement processes from day one so a trained coach can eventually deliver standard Tier 3.

### Partner dependency
If the peptide partner (the only currently-locked partner) becomes unavailable or terminates the relationship, Tier 3's clinical layer is materially affected. Mitigation: don't let any single partner be irreplaceable — once 2-3 partners are locked in similar domains, no single defection is fatal.

### Pricing risk
Tier 2 at $299-599 is bold for a market that hasn't seen the offering yet. If conversion is too low at $299+, the whole tier-2-as-bridge model breaks. Mitigation: launch the first cohort as a "founding members" rate (lower price, locked in for life), use it to validate willingness to pay before locking standard pricing.

### Regulatory exposure
Coordinating peptide and hormone protocols, even without prescribing, creates some exposure if not done carefully. Stay clear: Wylde never prescribes, never dispenses, never resells medications. All medical care is delivered by licensed practitioners under their own license. Wylde provides coaching and orchestration only.

### Capital / runway
Tier 3 generates real cash, but founder-led capacity caps at 3-5 clients = $50-125K quarterly maximum. If runway is tight, Tier 3 needs to start generating revenue within 30-60 days, not 90+. The 30-day milestone (1-3 engagements sold) is critical for runway protection.
